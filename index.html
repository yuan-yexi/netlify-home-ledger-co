How to Get It Going

Like all things worth doing, you'll need a plan, a strategy.

Financial Position

You can start off by gathering these data points:

  • Your CPF and cash positions will play a large part in the approcah you should take to acquire the right property. Log onto the CPF website to easily access this data.
  • Listing down any outstanding loans and liabilities. This will determine the leverage that your can maximize with banks. You can get this information from your bank.

Understand your Goals, the Market and Data

  • Research property value and trends by individual districts. This will help you understand the upside and risks involved.
  • Research on the mortgage options - a mortgage or financial calculator will be a great help.
  • Optimize your goals and budget. While having leverage is advantagous, it is important with any investment you undertake to not overstretch your financial positions.

Plan Out Your Strategy and Roadmap

Create a plan and execute. A plan provides more then a list of to-dos, it should also serve as a yardstick to ensure you're on the right track.

  • Once your goals are set and have an ideal location and property in mind, put it down.
  • Get out there and start hunting!

Going from Zero to Success

Here are 3 asset progression strategies you should consider.

  • Upgrading to A Bigger HDB

    The Good
    If the most important goal for you right now is having enough space for your growing family.

    • Good value for a larger space at a low psf
    • Lower financial commitment over course of ownership
    • A great choice to expand and not overstretch yourself

    The Pitfalls
    The truth is HDB flats are not a good "investment" if you're hoping to cash out somewhere down the road to benefit from some property windfall.

    • HDB prices have been largely stagnant
    • Not realistic to expect large appreciation that previous generation of HDB owners have experienced
    • CPF 2.5% compounded interest rates will likely outstrip the HDB value appreciation

    HDB Resale Price Index

  • Transitioning from HDB to Condo

    The Good
    Making good returns on your investment is your priority right now.

    • Much greater potential for capital appreciation
    • Historically proven to be a resilient asset class and strong recovery after major market shifts
    • Great choice for an upgrader or for first time investors

    The Pitfalls
    While usually the better investment option, it is not for everybody.

    • A higher monthly financial commitment in mortgage
    • Higher initial outlay
    • Consider costs such as MCST fees which can be sizable depending on the project

    Private Property Price Index

  • Sell 1 Buy 2

    The Good
    If you're looking for a strong passive income asset that also provide great potential for capital appreciation.

    • Maximise your leverage for higher returns
    • Rent as a sustainable passive income
    • Building a strong investment portfolio around property

    The Pitfalls
    This is a strategy that is not recommended unless you're in strong financial position.

    • Very high initial outlay
    • Consider the cost of ownership in terms of ongoing property management as a landlord
